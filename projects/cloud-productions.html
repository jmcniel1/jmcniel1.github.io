Context

Dr. Henry Cloud's Boundaries has sold close to twenty million copies. The challenge was never awareness — it was continuity. A book ends; growth doesn't. Boundaries.me had to take a body of work people already trusted and turn it into something they'd return to every day.

The audience arrives carrying something real — anxiety, a hard relationship, grief, codependency. The design couldn't read as clinical or self-helpy. It had to feel like a steady hand.

Approach

Warm, plain, unhurried. We built around the daily habit — the “One Thing” coaching video — and let the deeper library of courses and workshops sit behind it for when someone is ready to go further. Calm color, real photography, type that reads like a person talking rather than a brand performing.

The hierarchy always points to the next small step instead of the full catalog, so the platform meets people where they are rather than overwhelming them on day one.

Outcome

Boundaries.me runs as a membership — ninety-plus video courses with workbooks, daily coaching, live and archived workshops, and a members-only community — starting at $14.99 a month. The promise the brand makes is modest and durable: we'll walk with you, one thing at a time.
